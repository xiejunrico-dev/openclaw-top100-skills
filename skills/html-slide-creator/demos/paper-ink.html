— 01 —
The Problem

Presentations shouldn't require
a build system.

  • i. A complex toolchain — npm install, webpack configuration, three hundred megabytes of node_modules — merely to distribute a set of slides; this is the industry's quiet absurdity.
  • ii. Generic output: every AI-generated deck arrives bearing the same Inter typeface and purple gradients, stripped of all character and distinction.
  • iii. No ownership; only closed platforms, export locks, and monthly fees exacted for the privilege of accessing one's own content.
— 02 —
Discovery

Style discovery
that actually works.

You cannot describe design preferences in words; language proves insufficient for the task. slide-creator generates three visual previews — you react to them, rather than attempting to articulate your aesthetic intentions in prose.

I Ask mood. Four questions: topic, audience, energy, duration.
II Generate three previews. Rendered visually. React, not describe.
III Choose the one that feels right. The full presentation follows.

— 04 —
The Process

Three deliberate steps;
one finished presentation.

I

/slide-creator — Answer 4 questions

Topic, audience, mood, duration. The system proceeds from there.

II

Pick your style — React to 3 visual previews

No describing aesthetics in language. See, choose, proceed.

III

Open your presentation — Arrow keys · Space · Swipe

Pure HTML. Works without a server. Without a runtime. Without ceremony.

— 05 —
Capabilities

Designed for
genuine use.

Viewport Fitting
Every slide fits precisely within 100vh. No scrolling, ever. Every layout is conceived for the viewport it inhabits.
Inline Editing
Press E, click any text, Ctrl+S to save. An edit mode resides within every presentation, quietly waiting.
PPTX Export
/slide-creator --export pptx converts to PowerPoint, for those occasions when the boardroom insists upon it.
Two-Stage Workflow
--plan produces the outline; --generate produces the HTML. Review your intentions before committing them to form.
